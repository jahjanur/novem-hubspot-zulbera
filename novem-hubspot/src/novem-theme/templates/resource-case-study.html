From Reactive to Predictive: 18 Buildings, One Platform

How a North American institutional portfolio reduced emergency spend, improved insurance outcomes, and protected EBITDA — without replacing a single BMS.

A mid-size institutional owner operating 18 mixed-use properties across North America came to Novem Digital with a familiar problem: their buildings were generating data, but that data wasn't translating into decisions. Emergency callouts were running 40% over budget. Insurance renewals were increasingly painful. And when equipment failed, the first sign was usually an angry tenant call — not a dashboard alert.

Within 90 days of deploying the Novem Digital platform, the picture changed.

The Challenge

The portfolio's operators were experienced and capable — but they were flying blind. Building management systems captured data in silos. Maintenance logs lived in spreadsheets. Insurance claims were reviewed annually, not in real time. There was no single source of truth for what was at risk, what it would cost, and when.

The business consequence: reactive operations. Every emergency repair carried a 2–3× cost premium over planned maintenance. Capital planning was based on age of equipment, not condition or failure probability. And when renewals came around, the team couldn't produce the audit trail that underwriters increasingly demanded.

"We had data everywhere and insight nowhere. Novem gave us a way to turn that around without ripping out the systems we already had." — VP Operations, North American Institutional Portfolio

The Approach

Novem Digital connected to the portfolio's existing BMS, maintenance management, and insurance data — without requiring infrastructure replacement. The platform's risk intelligence layer normalized signals across 18 buildings, cross-referenced them against 20+ years of insurance and equipment loss data, and surfaced early warning flags ranked by financial exposure.

What the Platform Delivered

  • Complete asset inventory across all 18 properties — standardized and audit-ready
  • Predictive failure alerts ranked by TCRO impact, not just equipment age
  • Real-time risk dashboards aligned to capital planning and insurance renewal cycles
  • Evidence packages for underwriters — reducing renewal friction and improving terms
  • Operator-facing summaries that made risk visible without requiring data expertise

The Outcome

In the first 12 months, the portfolio saw a 34% reduction in unplanned emergency spend — not by adding headcount, but by acting earlier. Planned interventions replaced reactive callouts. Insurance renewal negotiations were supported by documented loss prevention activity for the first time, contributing to an 18% improvement in renewal outcomes.

Capital planning shifted from assumption-based to evidence-based. The team could show the board which assets were at risk, what the financial exposure was, and what the cost of intervention versus inaction looked like — all from a single platform.

"The ROI was clear by month four. But the more important shift was cultural — our operators started thinking in terms of prevention, not response." — Asset Manager, North American Institutional Portfolio
