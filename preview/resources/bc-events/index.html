The Challenge

A campus they couldn't
see inside.

A 184-acre campus — seven buildings, the oldest over 50 years old. No predictive visibility into aging infrastructure. Equipment failed without warning. Insurance claims mounted. Leadership were managing a campus they couldn't see inside.

"We knew equipment was failing, but we had no way to see it coming. Every breakdown meant scrambling for emergency repairs at premium rates."
— Operations Director

Millions of visitors. Seven buildings. Aging HVAC, ice-making equipment, refrigeration, lighting, and utilities — all operating without any predictive intelligence layer. When something failed, it was already too late.

The insurance risk was compounding every season. The real question wasn't whether equipment would fail. It was how much it would cost when it did.

The Solution

Predictive intelligence.
No rip-and-replace.

Novem layered predictive intelligence on top of existing infrastructure. Vibration sensors on ice-making equipment. Decibel monitoring across four parking lots. Water flow detection at the racetrack — a 10,000 litre/day leak found on day one. Weather and lightning APIs. Walk-in cooler condition monitoring.

Every sensor feeds into a single intelligence layer that detects anomalies, flags risk, and creates an auditable trail — without replacing a single piece of existing infrastructure.

In parallel: 7,000 incandescent lights replaced with programmable LED — 30% electrical savings, 4.5-year ROI. Campus-wide decibel monitoring eliminated noise complaints by producing real data proving truck traffic was the source, not the campus.

The result: a campus that could finally defend every capital and risk decision with data — and hold insurance premiums flat.

"That gas leak had been bleeding money for years, and we had no idea. The moment we installed the monitoring equipment, it became visible. That single discovery paid for the entire system."

— Operations Director, BC Events Campus
